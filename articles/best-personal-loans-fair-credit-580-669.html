Understanding "Fair Credit" – 580 to 669

Score Range Rating What It Means for Loans
300–579 Poor Very hard to get unsecured loans. Secured or credit-builder only.
580–669 Fair Some unsecured options available. Expect higher APRs (18–36%).
670–739 Good Most unsecured loans available. Competitive rates (10–18%).
740–850 Excellent Best rates (6–12%). All options available.

If you're in the 580–669 range, you're in the "subprime" category for most lenders. That means higher interest rates and smaller loan amounts—but you can still get approved.

How to Choose the Right Lender for Your Score

If your score is 580–599:

  • Try first: Avant (550 minimum) or Upgrade (560 minimum)
  • Second choice: OneMain (no minimum, but high rates)
  • Avoid: LendingClub, Best Egg (require 600+)

If your score is 600–639:

  • Try first: Upgrade or Avant (better rates than at 580)
  • Second choice: LendingClub or Best Egg (minimum 600 met)
  • Also consider: Upstart (if you have income/education)

If your score is 640–669:

  • Try first: All of the above, plus check local credit unions (often better rates)
  • Best rates likely: Upgrade or Best Egg (their low APRs become available)
  • Avoid: OneMain (you qualify for cheaper options)

Application Strategy: Don't Hurt Your Score

Step-by-step safe application process:

  1. Use soft pull pre-approval only – All lenders above offer this. It does NOT affect your credit score.
  2. Pre-approve with 2–3 lenders – Compare offers without harming your score.
  3. Pick the best offer and formally apply – This creates ONE hard inquiry (drops score 3–10 points temporarily).
  4. Do NOT apply to multiple lenders at once – Multiple hard inquiries for the same loan type within 14–45 days count as one for FICO, but why risk it?

Red flags to avoid:

  • ❌ Lenders who won't tell you rates without a hard pull
  • ❌ "No credit check guaranteed approval" (usually payday loans at 300%+ APR)
  • ❌ Upfront fees before approval (legitimate lenders deduct fees from loan amount)
  • ❌ Prepayment penalties (none of our recommended lenders have these)

Hidden Costs to Calculate Before You Sign

Example: $5,000 loan at 25% APR, 36 months, 5% origination fee.

  • Origination fee: $250 (taken from loan, so you receive $4,750)
  • Monthly payment: Approximately $199
  • Total interest over 3 years: Approximately $2,164
  • Total cost to borrow $4,750: $7,164 ($2,414 in fees + interest)

Effective APR including fees: Closer to 30–32%, not 25%.

Always ask: "What is my total repayment amount including all fees?"

FAQs

Can I get a $10,000 personal loan with a 580 credit score?

Yes, but expect high APRs (25–35%) and likely require a co-signer or collateral. OneMain and Avant are your best options.

Will applying for a loan improve my credit score?

Not directly. The hard inquiry lowers your score temporarily (3–10 points). However, making on-time payments over time WILL improve your score significantly.

How long should I wait after being denied?

Check your denial letter for the specific reason. Usually wait 3–6 months while fixing that issue (paying down utilization, disputing errors, etc.).

Do these lenders report to all 3 credit bureaus?

Yes – Upgrade, Avant, Upstart, LendingClub, OneMain, and Best Egg all report to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.
