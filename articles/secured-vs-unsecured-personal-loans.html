Secured vs. Unsecured Personal Loans: Which Builds Credit Faster?

Published: March 13, 2026 | Est. read time: 7 minutes

🏦 Bottom line up front: Secured loans are easier to get with bad credit, but unsecured loans report the same way. Both build credit identically—if you pay on time.

The Core Difference in 30 Seconds

Secured Loan Unsecured Loan
Collateral required? Yes (car, savings, property) No
Credit score needed 500–580+ 620+ typically
Interest rates 6%–18% 10%–36%
Risk to you Lose collateral if you default Lawsuit, wage garnishment
Credit building speed Same as unsecured Same as secured

What Is a Secured Personal Loan?

A secured loan requires you to pledge an asset as collateral. Common collateral types include:

  • Your car (auto title loan – usually predatory, avoid)
  • Savings account or CD (credit union secured loans – good)
  • Home equity (HELOC – but that's different product)
  • Cash balance (credit-builder loans use this model)

If you stop making payments, the lender can take your collateral. This lowers the lender's risk, which is why secured loans offer lower interest rates and higher approval odds for bad credit borrowers.

What Is an Unsecured Personal Loan?

An unsecured loan has no collateral. The lender approves you based solely on your credit history, income, and debt-to-income ratio. If you default, the lender cannot automatically take your property—but they can sue you, get a judgment, and garnish your wages.

Because the lender takes on more risk, unsecured loans have:

  • Higher interest rates (typically 10%–36% APR)
  • Higher credit score requirements (620+ for most)
  • Lower loan amounts ($1,000–$50,000 typically)

Which One Builds Credit Faster?

Neither. Credit bureaus don't know—and don't care—whether your loan is secured or unsecured. They only see:

  • Payment history (on time or late)
  • Account age
  • Loan balance relative to original amount
  • Credit mix (installment loan vs. revolving credit)

A secured loan from your credit union builds credit exactly the same as an unsecured loan from SoFi. The only difference is how easy it is to get approved.

📈 Key insight: FICO scoring models treat all installment loans identically regardless of collateral status.

Best Secured Loan Options for Credit Building (2026)

1. Credit Union Share-Secured Loans

Many credit unions offer loans backed by your savings account. You borrow against your own money. Rates as low as 2–3% above your savings dividend. Best option by far.

Example: Navy Federal, Alliant, or any local credit union.

2. Self – Credit Builder Loan

Technically secured by a CD they hold. No hard credit check. Reports to all 3 bureaus. $48/month average payment.

3. OneMain Financial Secured Loan

Requires collateral like a car (no older than 10 years). Rates high (18–36%) but approves scores as low as 550.

4. Local Banks (Chase, Wells Fargo)

Offer secured loans against CDs or savings. Rates good (6–10%) but require existing relationship.

Best Unsecured Loan Options for Credit Building (2026)

1. Upgrade

APR 8.49%–35.97%. Minimum credit 560. Reports to all 3 bureaus. Soft pull pre-approval.

2. Avant

APR 9.95%–35.99%. Minimum 550. Fast funding (next day). Origination fee 0–4.75%.

3. Upstart

APR 5.2%–35.99%. Uses AI (considers education + job history). Minimum 500 but prefers 600+.

4. LendingClub

APR 8.98%–35.99%. Peer-to-peer model. Minimum 600. Reports to all bureaus.

Which One Should You Choose?

Choose a Secured Loan If:

  • ✓ Your credit score is below 620
  • ✓ You have savings or a car you can use as collateral
  • ✓ You want the lowest possible interest rate
  • ✓ You're certain you can make payments (or you'll lose collateral)
  • ✓ You're a credit union member with share-secured option

Choose an Unsecured Loan If:

  • ✓ Your credit score is 640+
  • ✓ You don't want to risk any asset
  • ✓ You need money quickly (unsecured often funds in 1–3 days)
  • ✓ You want a larger loan amount ($10k+)
  • ✓ You prefer no collateral paperwork

Real Example: Two Borrowers, Same Credit Score

  • Monthly payment: $174
  • Total interest: $88
  • Used car as collateral
  • Paid on time every month
  • Score after 12 months: 580 → 638 (+58 points)

Borrower B: Unsecured Loan ($2,000, 12 months, 18% APR)

  • Total interest: $196
  • No collateral
  • Paid on time every month

Conclusion: The secured borrower saved $108 in interest but saw nearly identical credit improvement. Both loans worked equally well for building credit.

FAQs

Do secured loans show up differently on my credit report?

No. Both appear as "installment loan." The secured status is not visible to other lenders or credit scoring models.

Can I get a secured loan with no credit check?

Some credit unions offer "credit builder" secured loans with no hard pull. Self and Kikoff also use soft pulls only.

Which builds credit faster for someone with no score?

Equal. The first account of any type (secured or unsecured) generates a FICO score within 6 months of on-time payments.

Can I convert a secured loan to unsecured?

Rarely. Some lenders allow "graduation" after 12–24 on-time payments, but this is uncommon with personal loans (more common with secured credit cards).

Final Verdict

For credit building specifically: Secured and unsecured loans work identically. Choose based on your approval odds and interest rate, not based on credit building speed.

Our recommendation for most readers: If your score is below 620, start with a secured loan (credit union share-secured or Self). If your score is 640+, compare unsecured offers from Upgrade or Avant to avoid tying up collateral.
