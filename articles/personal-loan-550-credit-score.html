How to Get a Personal Loan With a 550 Credit Score – 5 Lenders That Approve

Published: March 20, 2026 | Est. read time: 7 minutes

🔑 Bottom line: Yes, you can get a personal loan with a 550 credit score. But expect high APRs (25–36%) and possible collateral requirements. Here's exactly where to apply.

A 550 score is in the "poor" category. Traditional banks (Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America) will likely decline you. But specialized subprime lenders and credit unions exist for exactly this situation.

5 Lenders That Approve 550 Credit Scores

2. OneMain Financial – Best for Secured Loans (Use Car as Collateral)

Why OneMain: If Avant denies you, OneMain will approve with collateral (a car less than 10 years old). You keep the car while paying the loan. Default = repossession.

3. Upgrade – Good if Your Score Is Closer to 560

4. Credit Unions – Best for Relationship Approvals

Credit unions are non-profit and often approve members with bad credit if you have an existing relationship (direct deposit, savings account).

  • Navy Federal Credit Union (military/DoD) – known for bad credit approvals
  • PenFed Credit Union – open to anyone with $5 donation to qualifying organization
  • Local credit unions – walk in and talk to a human

Before You Apply: Do These 3 Things First

1. Check Your Credit Report for Errors

At 550, you likely have negative items. But sometimes errors drop your score. Pull free reports from AnnualCreditReport.com. Dispute any accounts not yours or late payments marked incorrectly.

2. Consider a Credit-Builder Loan Instead

If you don't need cash immediately, a credit-builder loan (like Self) requires no credit check and builds your score over 12 months. After that, you'll qualify for better APRs (10–18% instead of 30%+).

3. Get a Co-signer If Possible

If someone you trust has good credit (670+), adding them as a co-signer dramatically increases approval odds and lowers your APR by 10–20%. But they are legally responsible if you default.

Step-by-Step: Apply With a 550 Score

  1. Start with Avant – Use their soft pull pre-approval. No score impact.
  2. If denied, try Upgrade – Also soft pull. Worth a shot.
  3. If both deny, try OneMain – Be prepared to offer collateral (car).
  4. If you have a credit union account, go there – In-person approvals are more flexible.
  5. If all else fails, take a credit-builder loan – Build credit for 12 months, then reapply for a real personal loan.

Avoid at all costs: Payday loans, title loans, or any lender promising "guaranteed approval no credit check." These have APRs of 300%+ and will trap you in debt.

How to Raise Your Score From 550 to 650 in 6–12 Months

Quick summary for 550:

  • Open a secured credit card – Discover it Secured ($200 deposit). Use for small purchases, pay in full.
  • Open a credit-builder loan – Self or Credit Strong ($25–$50/month).
  • Pay all existing bills on time – No exceptions. One late payment destroys progress.
  • Dispute any errors on your report – Remove inaccurate collections or late payments.
  • Keep credit utilization under 10% – On secured card, use only $20/month of a $200 limit.

After 6–12 months of this, your score will likely be 620–680. Then you qualify for much better loans.

FAQs

Can I get a $5,000 personal loan with a 550 credit score?

Possibly from OneMain with collateral. Without collateral, Avant or Upgrade may approve $2,000–$3,000, but $5,000 is harder. Credit unions are your best bet for higher amounts.

Will applying hurt my credit score?

Soft pull pre-approvals (Avant, Upgrade) do NOT hurt. Only formal applications create hard inquiries (-5 to -10 points). Use soft pulls first to check approval odds.

What if I have a bankruptcy on my report?

If discharged 2+ years ago, OneMain and some credit unions will still approve. If less than 2 years, focus on credit-builder products first.

Should I use a co-signer?

Yes – if you have someone willing. It's the single best way to get approved and lower your APR. Just don't ruin their credit by missing payments.

Final Recommendation

If you need money urgently (emergency only): Apply to Avant → OneMain (with collateral) → credit union. Expect 25–36% APR. Borrow only what you absolutely need.

If you can wait 6–12 months: Don't take a high-APR loan. Use a credit-builder loan and secured credit card to raise your score. Then apply for a normal personal loan at 10–18% APR. You'll save hundreds or thousands in interest.
