What Is a Credit-Building Loan? (And 3 Places to Get One)

Published: March 12, 2026 | Updated: Monthly

A credit-building loan is designed for one purpose: improving your credit score. Unlike a traditional personal loan, these products focus on reporting your on-time payments to all three credit bureaus—Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.

How Credit-Building Loans Work

Most credit-building loans use a "secured" structure:

  1. You apply and get approved (often with no hard credit check)
  2. The lender places the loan amount in a locked savings account
  3. You make monthly payments over 12–24 months
  4. At the end, you receive the money minus fees

Your payments are reported to credit bureaus every month. Make all payments on time, and your score typically rises 30–60 points.

Who Should Get One?

  • ✓ You have no credit history (thin file)
  • ✓ Your credit score is 500–620
  • ✓ You want to build credit without a credit card
  • ✓ You have $25–$50 per month available for payments

Alternatives to Consider

If you already have a 620+ score, a secured credit card might be cheaper. But for raw score improvement, credit-building loans report positive payment history more consistently.
